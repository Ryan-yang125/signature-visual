# Failure Signatures

Diagnose the visible symptom, identify the design or process layer that caused it, and revise that layer before adding finish. Use the smallest search response allowed by the scope tier.

## Creative-search failures

### Taxonomy-first convergence

**Symptoms:** candidates quickly become a field, specimen, instrument, archive, topology, or another familiar packaged family; project nouns appear mainly in labels.

**Cause:** examples, Pattern Language, renderer demos, or artifact categories were consulted before the target evidence generated a rule.

**Revision:** close the taxonomy, return to the raw evidence kernel, and produce an evidence-derived rule. Landmark work adds or replaces the taxonomy-external direction.

### Obvious-attractor capture

**Symptoms:** independent runs literalize the product name, repeat the same category diagram, or select the first noun-to-artifact metaphor; a direction receives a taxonomy-external label while its silhouette remains predictable.

**Cause:** the natural translation entered selection as a full candidate, and the search had no per-run coordinate when prior-output history was unavailable.

**Revision:** record the natural translation as a shadow baseline, run the bundled creative-offset generator, and author anti-literal plus offset directions. A baseline-derived winner needs a second-order transform across at least three structural axes.

### Ritual branching

**Symptoms:** several proposals exist, yet the extra candidates carry little uncertainty or learning; names and finishes differ more than structures.

**Cause:** candidate count became a compliance target.

**Revision:** apply the scope tier. Stop a section search when its first direction passes distance and fit. Continue landmark search only while candidates reveal distinct worlds or unresolved risk.

### Tuning presented as distance

**Symptoms:** candidates change palette, count, intensity, easing, camera angle, or renderer while thumbnails and causal sequences remain equivalent.

**Cause:** the branch changed tuning axes inside one governing rule.

**Revision:** use the candidate distance matrix. Rebranch on silhouette, spatial grammar, material causality, temporal archetype, interaction meaning, or type role.

### Landmark without an external direction

**Symptoms:** every landmark proposal can be traced to a packaged pattern, common artifact stance, or trend reference.

**Cause:** search never began from a target-specific primitive outside the available taxonomy.

**Revision:** generate one direction using only target data, behavior, language, object relations, constraints, and production traces. Record its origin before opening Pattern Language.

### Section over-search

**Symptoms:** a supporting section receives a full identity exercise, several polished alternates, and heavy evaluation while the page job is already solved.

**Cause:** landmark search depth was applied to local work.

**Revision:** retain the strongest candidate, run source/output distance, and branch only on a recorded failure.

### Refinement amnesia

**Symptoms:** a refinement discards a useful thesis, replaces the whole metaphor, or generates unrelated concepts before diagnosing the current failure.

**Cause:** refinement was treated as greenfield direction.

**Revision:** restore the confirmed thesis and signature rule. Compare current and proposed fingerprints, then change the smallest responsible structural layer.

### Output family resemblance

**Symptoms:** the result differs from its direct reference yet resembles recent work through the same silhouette, editorial plate, material metaphor, annotation density, temporal grammar, or interaction pattern.

**Cause:** originality review measured source distance and skipped output distance.

**Revision:** name the nearest accessible prior output in the design record and change the shared generating rule. Record `unavailable` with the search performed when history cannot be inspected.

### Score laundering

**Symptoms:** a criterion receives 5 through adjectives, intentions, or self-assessment; visible flaws and counterevidence are absent.

**Cause:** evaluation lacks anchored evidence and independent review.

**Revision:** cap the score at 4 until artifact evidence, the strongest counterevidence, and an independent reviewer support the 5.

### Record bloat

**Symptoms:** the design record becomes a process transcript, stores every discarded idea, or duplicates the handoff.

**Cause:** machine-readable history was confused with creative documentation.

**Revision:** retain the compact schema fields: `schemaVersion`, `tier`, `surface`, `thesis`, `directions`, `distanceMatrix`, `selection`, `signatureRule`, `renderer`, `states`, `runtimeCapabilities`, and `revision`.

## Direction failures

### Explain-first visual

**Symptoms:** copy promises trust, intelligence, resilience, or coordination while the frame remains generic without explanation.

**Cause:** the thesis names a mood and supplies no observable rule.

**Revision:** connect target evidence to topology, density, mapping, material causality, composition, or state change that can be challenged in a still or semantic sequence.

### Renderer swap, same design

**Symptoms:** Canvas, Three.js, WebGL, and SVG versions preserve the same split layout, silhouette, labels, palette, and interaction.

**Cause:** technology supplied novelty after the direction had already collapsed.

**Revision:** return to the distance failure and change the governing rule. Route to an engine again after freeze.

### Familiar demo fingerprint

**Symptoms:** wireframe blob, aurora shader, constellation particles, glowing node graph, cursor lens, auto-orbit camera, or starter-specific composition dominates.

**Cause:** starter geometry, material, timing, or semantics survived implementation.

**Revision:** preserve lifecycle infrastructure and replace the visual program: silhouette, emitter or topology, governing equation, density event, material model, temporal structure, and semantic input.

### Surface remix

**Symptoms:** a reference's composition, color relationship, typography, and temporal behavior remain recognizable after parameter edits.

**Cause:** study captured objects and styling instead of portable principles.

**Revision:** write the source fingerprint, cite the nearest traits, and change the structural cause behind them. Preserve a recognizable surface trait only when target evidence requires it.

### Spectacle without a subject

**Symptoms:** activity fills the surface while no object, relation, event, document, environment, or material sample carries the idea.

**Cause:** the resting frame and page job were skipped.

**Revision:** name what the visitor encounters, author its strongest held composition, and remove activity that has no role in the governing rule.

## Composition failures

### Wallpaper field

**Symptoms:** uniform detail, weak negative space, and interchangeable activity.

**Revision:** create one target-derived density event, shape a void or dominant relation, crop deliberately, and subordinate secondary frequencies.

### Split-screen habit

**Symptoms:** copy repeatedly occupies one side while an effect fills the other across unrelated projects.

**Revision:** derive the text relationship from the content axis. Consider framing, annotation, sequence, threshold, central aperture, edge crop, or another target-specific arrangement.

### Motion-dependent frame

**Symptoms:** screenshots look empty or accidental; interest appears only after watching.

**Revision:** author a complete held frame. Let time expose behavior or consequence.

### Mobile shrinkage

**Symptoms:** tiny focal object, clipped labels, lost safe zone, or a miniature desktop scene.

**Revision:** change anchor, crop, layer count, label strategy, and density while preserving the governing rule.

## Material failures

### Generic deep-tech finish

**Symptoms:** dark navy, violet/cyan gradient, additive glow, grid, and corner telemetry appear without target cause.

**Revision:** define a substance, light model, edge hierarchy, and texture attachment from the selected direction. Reserve signal treatments for meaningful state.

### Effects stack

**Symptoms:** bloom, grain, scan lines, chromatic separation, blur, iridescence, and vignette compete.

**Revision:** keep one primary material behavior and one controlled support. Strengthen the silhouette and causal surface response.

### Uniform edge

**Symptoms:** every object shares one opacity, glow, blur, or line weight.

**Revision:** assign edge behavior to primary silhouette, internal structure, history or atmosphere, and active response.

## Temporal and interaction failures

### Default five-act motion

**Symptoms:** unrelated visuals all rest, wake, build, peak, and recover with similar timing.

**Cause:** a universal dramatic arc replaced subject-derived temporal structure.

**Revision:** select or author a temporal archetype from the governing rule. Use held, one-shot, finite-state, event-response, scroll, ambient, accretive, live, or another fitting structure.

### Screensaver time

**Symptoms:** perpetual drift, spin, wave, or noise has no consequence.

**Revision:** give time a job tied to state, causality, history, control, or live data. A held state may communicate more.

### Same ease everywhere

**Symptoms:** position, opacity, geometry, camera, and labels start and settle together.

**Revision:** derive latency and damping from material and causality. Synchronize only events that share a cause.

### Interaction as cursor chase

**Symptoms:** objects follow, repel, or glow around input without revealing product behavior.

**Revision:** map input to a semantic role and show its bounded consequence. Remove interaction when observation is clearer.

### Broken aftermath

**Symptoms:** pointer exit strands geometry, brightness snaps, scroll back creates an invalid state, or irreversible action silently resets.

**Revision:** define exit, cancellation, reversibility, persistence, and teardown according to the temporal archetype.

## Technical failures

### Non-deterministic review

**Symptoms:** captures vary across runs and comparisons cannot isolate revisions.

**Revision:** seed random state, expose semantic time/data/input controls, and save a capture manifest.

### Empty reduced-motion state

**Symptoms:** trails vanish, paths have zero length, a shader becomes blank, or meaning disappears.

**Revision:** render a complete held or resolved composition with the same thesis and hierarchy.

### Gallery GPU pile-up

**Symptoms:** offscreen scenes animate, mobile heats up, or scrolling stutters.

**Revision:** initialize near visibility, cap DPR and density, pause offscreen, show posters where useful, and dispose resources.

### Lifecycle leak

**Symptoms:** duplicated animation after navigation, retained context, or listeners firing on removed nodes.

**Revision:** centralize ownership, make cleanup idempotent, release every observer, listener, frame, timer, and GPU resource, and test repeated mount/dispose.

## Escalation order

1. real-surface evidence and thesis validity;
2. candidate, source, and output distance appropriate to the scope;
3. resting composition and governing rule;
4. material, temporal, and interaction causality;
5. renderer and production resilience;
6. finish and micro-detail.

Section and refinement work return only to the earliest failed layer. Landmark work can reopen wider search when identity or structural distance remains unresolved.
