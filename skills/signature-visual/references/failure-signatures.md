# Failure Signatures

Diagnose the visible symptom, identify the design layer that caused it, and revise that layer before adding polish.

## Template-distance failures

### Renderer swap, same design

**Symptoms:** every case uses the same copy/effect split, typography, labels, palette, and interaction while only Canvas/Three/WebGL/SVG changes.

**Cause:** routing began from technology and the surrounding design shell supplied the identity.

**Revision:** return to three Direction Cards. Change artifact stance, spatial archetype, material substance, temporal character, and type role across them. Select through the product thesis.

### Familiar demo fingerprint

**Symptoms:** wireframe blob, aurora shader, constellation particles, glowing node graph, rainbow noise, cursor lens, auto-orbit camera.

**Cause:** a starter's default visual program survived implementation.

**Revision:** preserve lifecycle code only. Replace silhouette/emitter, governing equation, density event, material model, phase arc, and semantic input mapping.

### Surface remix

**Symptoms:** a reference's composition, color relationship, typography, and motion remain recognizable after minor parameter edits.

**Cause:** study recorded objects and styling rather than portable principles.

**Revision:** write a source fingerprint and transformation matrix. Change at least three axes and preserve a maximum of one recognizable surface trait with direct target meaning.

## Direction failures

### Explain-first visual

**Symptoms:** the copy says coordination, trust, intelligence, or resilience; the visual remains generic without the explanation.

**Cause:** the thesis names a mood rather than an observable phenomenon.

**Revision:** choose a native product tension, paired verbs, and a physical/spatial rule. Encode the rule in topology, density, persistence, or state change.

### Three directions that share one skeleton

**Symptoms:** directions differ through palette, intensity, particle count, or post effects while thumbnails look alike.

**Cause:** variations were generated inside one fingerprint.

**Revision:** require different spatial archetypes and at least four changed axes. Name directions after subject-specific phenomena.

### Spectacle without an artifact

**Symptoms:** activity fills the viewport yet lacks a clear subject, object, document, or event.

**Cause:** the artifact stance and resting composition were skipped.

**Revision:** choose environment, specimen, instrument, document, event, or material sample; build its strongest still frame first.

## Composition failures

### Wallpaper field

**Symptoms:** uniform detail, no focal density, weak negative space, effect feels interchangeable.

**Revision:** add one dominant density event, shape a void, crop deliberately, and subordinate secondary frequencies.

### Split-screen habit

**Symptoms:** copy repeatedly occupies one side and an effect fills the other, across unrelated projects.

**Revision:** explore monument, plate, threshold, topology, and sequence archetypes; choose a text relationship other than protected side-by-side when evidence supports it.

### Motion-dependent frame

**Symptoms:** screenshots look empty or accidental; the visual gains interest only after watching for several seconds.

**Revision:** author rest as a complete poster. Use motion to reveal behavior and change, rather than to fill compositional gaps.

### Mobile shrinkage

**Symptoms:** tiny focal object, clipped labels, lost safe zone, miniature desktop scene.

**Revision:** change anchor, crop, layer count, annotation rail, and density. Preserve the signature rule and main silhouette.

## Material failures

### Generic deep-tech finish

**Symptoms:** dark navy, violet/cyan gradient, mono labels, grid, additive glow, corner telemetry.

**Revision:** select a specific substance and artifact. Assign ground/body/signal/exception roles, remove decorative instrumentation, and make texture belong to a physical layer.

### Effects stack

**Symptoms:** bloom, noise, scan lines, chromatic separation, blur, iridescence, and vignette compete.

**Revision:** keep one primary finish and one subtle support. Strengthen silhouette and substance with direct lighting or edge behavior.

### Uniform edge

**Symptoms:** everything has the same glow, opacity, line weight, or sharpness.

**Revision:** define edge hierarchy for primary silhouette, internal structure, atmosphere/history, and active response.

## Motion failures

### Screensaver time

**Symptoms:** perpetual drift, spin, wave, or noise with no development or consequence.

**Revision:** author rest, wake, develop, peak, and recover. Make topology, density, or material state change at the peak.

### Same ease everywhere

**Symptoms:** camera, geometry, opacity, and signals start and settle together; motion feels synthetic and flat.

**Revision:** assign material-specific latency and damping. Coordinate one peak while allowing secondary systems to lead or trail.

### Interaction as cursor chase

**Symptoms:** objects follow, repel, or glow around the pointer without product meaning.

**Revision:** interpret input as probe, pressure, alignment, sample, route, resource, or tool. Show consequence and recovery through the system model.

### Broken recovery

**Symptoms:** pointer exit strands geometry, brightness snaps, scroll back produces an invalid state.

**Revision:** derive state from a bounded controller, handle exit/cancel, and capture released plus recovered frames.

## Technical failures

### Non-deterministic review

**Symptoms:** screenshots vary across runs; critique cannot compare the same moment.

**Revision:** seed random state, expose time/progress/input controls, use fixed-step simulation or checkpoints, and save a capture manifest.

### Empty reduced-motion state

**Symptoms:** trails disappear, lines have zero length, shader is black, or the idea vanishes when animation stops.

**Revision:** render an authored key state with complete marks and the same signature rule. Reduced motion receives its own composition choice.

### Gallery GPU pile-up

**Symptoms:** several canvases or scenes animate offscreen, mobile heats up, scrolling stutters.

**Revision:** show posters first, initialize near visibility, run one high-cost scene at a time, cap DPR, and dispose on removal.

### Lifecycle leak

**Symptoms:** duplicated animation after navigation, retained context, listeners firing on removed nodes.

**Revision:** centralize ownership, make cleanup idempotent, release every observer/listener/resource, and test repeated mount/dispose.

## Escalation order

When several failures appear, revise in this order:

1. thesis and product specificity;
2. artifact stance and composition;
3. motion phases and semantic interaction;
4. material hierarchy;
5. renderer technique and performance;
6. finish and micro-detail.

Later layers can amplify a sound direction. They rarely repair an unresolved thesis or frame.
