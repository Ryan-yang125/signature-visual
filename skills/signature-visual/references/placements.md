# Placement grammar

Placement determines whether a visual feels integrated or attached. Choose one page role before selecting a recipe.

## Hero focal

Use for a product idea that benefits from a single object or system people can understand quickly.

- Occupy 35–60% of the hero area.
- Establish a text-safe zone before composing the visual.
- Let the object crop intentionally at one or two edges.
- Keep interaction readable from the resting frame.
- Use a composed mobile crop or simplified object.

Strong matches: living orb, voxel bloom, orbital instrument.

## Ambient field

Use to create atmosphere around existing content.

- Keep contrast low behind text.
- Concentrate detail away from copy and controls.
- Use slow base motion and bounded pointer response.
- Let the field continue beyond the container so edges feel natural.
- Pause when outside the viewport.

Strong matches: particle current, watercolor drift, radiance field.

## Section artifact

Use to explain one concept between content sections.

- Connect the animation to the section’s claim.
- Give the artifact a clear entry and a complete resting state.
- Use scroll progress when the sequence has semantic order.
- Keep supporting labels short and real.

Strong matches: resonance matrix, spatial lineage, signal weave.

## Technical surface

Use when the product world includes systems, measurement, networks, simulation, or infrastructure.

- Let labels encode meaningful states or concepts.
- Use geometry and hierarchy to teach.
- Preserve crisp lines across device sizes.
- Separate decorative marks from accessible semantic labels.

Strong matches: meteorological scan, orbital instrument, spatial lineage.

## Transition

Use for a memorable change between two meaningful states.

- Tie progress to scroll or route state.
- Design both endpoints as stable compositions.
- Keep the animation reversible.
- Preserve navigation and reading position.

Strong matches: phase membrane, assembly field, contour reveal.

## Cursor layer

Use as a restrained accent when the page already has a strong layout.

- Keep the effect local to meaningful regions.
- Preserve normal cursor and control behavior.
- Let the layer settle quickly after input ends.
- Disable or reinterpret it for coarse pointers.

Strong matches: magnetic threads, spectral lens, local ripple.
