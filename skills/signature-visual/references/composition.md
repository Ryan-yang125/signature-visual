# Composition

Design the resting frame before tuning motion. Computational visuals earn presence through scale, crop, negative space, and their relationship to content.

## Frame model

Declare these values for the selected direction:

```text
FRAME        viewport / section / object / inset artifact / edge transition
ANCHOR       center / edge / baseline / copy axis / data coordinate
SILHOUETTE   mass, void, contour, or repeated rhythm visible at thumbnail size
LAYERS       background / field / subject / annotation / content
CROP         contained / grazing / severed / off-canvas continuation
SAFE ZONE    shape and location, plus behavior under interaction
TYPE ROLE    content / annotation / frame / texture / subject
TENSION      the deliberate imbalance or interruption
```

## Six spatial archetypes

Use these as starting structures, then make the proportions project-specific:

1. **Monument** — one dominant subject with surrounding quiet.
2. **Field** — distributed activity with a designed density gradient.
3. **Plate** — an inset artifact, document, specimen card, or instrument.
4. **Threshold** — activity crosses an edge, seam, horizon, or section boundary.
5. **Topology** — relationships and paths create the main silhouette.
6. **Sequence** — several frames or panels reveal comparison, time, or causality.

Direction Cards should avoid sharing the same archetype unless the brief imposes it.

## Thumbnail test

Capture the frame at 160 px wide and inspect:

- one dominant mass or rhythm remains visible;
- text and visual each own a clear territory;
- density has an intentional high and low region;
- the crop appears deliberate;
- the frame does not depend on small labels or glow for interest.

Blur the frame heavily. The tonal hierarchy should still match the content hierarchy.

## Text relationships

Choose one:

- **Protected:** activity falls away from a fixed text-safe shape.
- **Framed:** the visual builds a boundary or aperture around text.
- **Occluded:** controlled overlap creates depth while contrast remains stable.
- **Annotated:** text labels the visual as an artifact or system.
- **Sequential:** copy and visual trade priority across scroll or phases.

Do not default to copy on the left and effect on the right. Explore vertical stacking, central apertures, edge crops, full-bleed type, inset instruments, and multi-panel sequences when the page supports them.

## Typography relationships

Typography can stay outside the visual or become part of its governing system:

- **Content:** normal page hierarchy remains primary while the visual supports it.
- **Annotation:** labels, values, and captions establish a document or instrument stance.
- **Frame:** large type creates edges, apertures, or cropping relationships.
- **Texture:** real project language repeats to form terrain, density, or history.
- **Subject:** glyphs, words, or variable-type axes carry the core transformation.

Derive typeface, scale contrast, and label behavior from the project or chosen artifact. Monospace labels communicate a specific production vernacular; they should not appear automatically around every computational visual. Keep real language readable in at least one authored state.

## Density choreography

Use density to create meaning:

- convergence indicates decision, trust, or coordination;
- erosion indicates loss, uncertainty, or passage;
- stratification indicates history or layers;
- sparse anomalies indicate discovery or risk;
- a dense boundary with a quiet interior indicates protection;
- periodic clustering indicates rhythm or collective behavior.

Keep one dominant density event. Even distribution often reads as wallpaper.

## Mobile re-composition

Author a mobile frame with the same signature rule:

- move the anchor to support the shorter reading distance;
- simplify layers before shrinking every element;
- preserve the silhouette and primary density event;
- convert side annotations into a top/bottom rail or accessible disclosure;
- enlarge meaningful interaction targets;
- choose a stable still frame when continuous motion adds little on touch.

Record desktop and mobile anchor/crop values in the design specification.
